The India-specific gap

India's rural healthcare has no shared memory

ASHA workers, PHCs, private clinics, and district hospitals each hold a fragment of a patient's story — usually on paper, usually lost between visits.

Today, without shared memory

  • An elderly or illiterate patient often can't recall their own diagnosis, allergy, or last prescription.
  • A district hospital repeats tests the PHC already ran days earlier.
  • A drug allergy noted at one clinic is invisible to the next — a real safety risk.
  • ASHA workers' home-visit notes rarely reach the doctor who sees the patient next.

With Setu Swasth

  • Every visit — home, PHC, hospital, pharmacy — is remembered permanently under the patient's own ID.
  • Any provider can recall the full history in seconds, in plain language.
  • Allergies and past diagnoses surface automatically, never left to memory.
  • The record improves and stays clean over time — corrected or erased on request, never silently lost.
Built directly on Cognee's memory lifecycle

Four Cognee calls, one continuous patient record

Every patient gets their own Cognee dataset (their patient ID). These are the exact calls this app makes — visible live in the demo's Memory Lens.

01

remember()

Every visit note is ingested via cognee.remember(text, session_id=patient_id) and structured into the graph.

02

recall()

A provider's question is answered via cognee.recall(query, session_id=patient_id), routed across semantic + graph search.

03

improve()

cognee.improve() — memify — prunes stale entries and sharpens the graph as a patient's history grows.

04

forget()

cognee.forget(dataset=patient_id) lets a clinic correct or erase a record — memory is a choice, never a leak.
